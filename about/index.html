About Murthy Malapaka

Moving Enterprises Beyond Labor-Centric Operating Models

I believe the next era of enterprise transformation will not be defined by deploying more AI. It will be defined by the transition from labor-centric operating models to AI-native operating models.

Over the past three decades, I have worked through every major enterprise technology wave — client-server, internet, cloud, automation, and now AI. Each wave reinforced a consistent lesson: technology creates its greatest value when it changes the operating model, not when it merely improves existing processes.

AI represents a fundamentally different shift. It enables enterprises to rethink how work itself is produced — moving beyond models where human labor remains the primary mechanism of delivery, toward operating models powered by digital labor, intelligent agents, enterprise knowledge, platform intelligence, and autonomous execution.

Most recently, I led the Research & Development organization at SLK Software responsible for defining and delivering PeakPerform™, an AI-native autonomous operations platform combining Agentic AI, Digital Labor, Assisted AI, operational intelligence, observability, and enterprise-scale augmentation techniques.

My work has focused on one central question:

How do enterprises redesign operating models so that work is eliminated, simplified, or autonomously executed — instead of continuously adding more labor to manage complexity?

Eliminate First. Automate Second.

“Eliminate First. Automate Second.” is not simply an automation principle. It is a design principle for building AI-native operating models.

Before automating work, organizations should first ask whether the work should exist at all, whether the platform already has the intelligence to resolve it, and whether human intervention is truly necessary.

This approach has helped organizations achieve significant operating model transformation, including 60–80% work elimination, 35%+ cost takeout, improved service quality, reduced MTTR, and a shift from reactive operations to proactive and autonomous execution.
